# Position Sizing: How Much Should You Trade?

**Category:** Trading Strategies  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Reading Time:** 10 minutes

## The Most Important Decision

Position sizing isn't about WHAT to trade - it's about HOW MUCH.

**Wrong approach:** "I'll buy $5,000 of Bitcoin"  
**Right approach:** "I'll risk $100 on this Bitcoin trade"

## Why Position Sizing Matters More Than Entry

### Scenario: Two Traders, Same Analysis

**Trader A:**
- Has $10,000
- Buys $9,000 of Bitcoin (90% of capital)
- No stop loss
- Price drops 20%
- Loss: $1,800
- Remaining: $8,200
- Needs 22% gain to break even

**Trader B:**
- Has $10,000
- Buys $1,000 of Bitcoin (10% of capital)
- Stop loss at 5%
- Price drops 20%, sells at 5%
- Loss: $50
- Remaining: $9,950
- Can try 199 more times

**Who survives?** Trader B can make 199 mistakes. Trader A can make 5.

## The 1% Rule (Beginner-Friendly)

**Never risk more than 1% of your capital on a single trade.**

### Calculation:
```
Capital: $10,000
Max risk per trade: 1% = $100

You want to buy Bitcoin at $50,000
Stop loss at $47,500 (5% drop)

Position size calculation:
$100 risk ÷ 5% price drop = $2,000 position
Buy: $2,000 worth of Bitcoin

If stopped out: Lose only $100 (1%)
If 20% drop: Total loss capped at $100
```

## The 2% Rule (Intermediate)

More aggressive, still safe:
- Risk 2% per trade
- With $10,000, risk $200
- Can lose 50 trades before account blown
- Still very conservative

## The Kelly Criterion (Advanced)

**Formula:** Position Size = (Win Rate × Avg Win - Loss Rate × Avg Loss) / Avg Win

Don't worry about the math - the simulator calculates this for you based on your history.

## Common Position Sizing Mistakes

### ❌ Mistake 1: Equal Dollar Amounts
```
"I'll put $1,000 in every trade"

Problem:
- Bitcoin at $50,000 → Risk might be $500
- Altcoin at $1 → Risk might be $100
- Same dollar, different risk!
```

### ❌ Mistake 2: "Going All In"
```
"This is a sure thing, 100% of capital!"

Reality:
- No trade is certain
- One loss = account blown
- No recovery possible
```

### ❌ Mistake 3: Increasing Size After Losses
```
Lost $100 → "I'll win it back with $200 trade"
Lost $200 → "Now I'll risk $400"
Lost $400 → "All in!"
Account blown
```

This is called **revenge trading** - the fastest way to zero.

### ❌ Mistake 4: Percentage of Capital Instead of Risk

```
Wrong: "I'll use 10% of capital = $1,000"
Right: "I'll risk 1% of capital = $100"

The difference:
- First puts $1,000 at full risk
- Second only risks $100 with stop loss
```

## Position Sizing by Experience Level

### Beginner (You)
- Risk: 0.5% - 1% per trade
- Max positions: 3 open trades
- Focus: Survival and learning

### Intermediate
- Risk: 1% - 2% per trade
- Max positions: 5 open trades
- Focus: Consistency

### Advanced
- Risk: 2% - 3% per trade
- Max positions: 10 open trades
- Focus: Optimization

## Leverage and Position Sizing

**Leverage multiplies EVERYTHING:**

```
Without leverage:
- Buy $1,000 Bitcoin
- 10% drop = $100 loss

With 10x leverage:
- Control $10,000 Bitcoin with $1,000
- 10% drop = $1,000 loss (account wiped!)
```

**Rule:** If using leverage, reduce position size proportionally.

```
Normal: Risk 1% = $100
With 5x leverage: Risk 0.2% = $20
With 10x leverage: Risk 0.1% = $10
```

## Scaling: When to Increase Position Size

**Only increase when:**
- [ ] You have 50+ trades logged
- [ ] Win rate above 50%
- [ ] 3+ months of consistent profits
- [ ] Still using stop losses
- [ ] Emotionally stable

**Never increase because:**
- You had 2 winning trades
- You "feel confident"
- You want to make money faster
- You're behind on goals

## Mental Models

### The Turtle Approach
- Slow and steady
- Small positions
- Survive first, profit second
- Compound over time

### The Sniper Approach
- Wait for perfect setups
- Larger positions on best opportunities
- Very selective
- High win rate required

**For beginners:** Be the turtle.

## Your Action Plan

1. Calculate your risk per trade (1% recommended)
2. Set stop loss FIRST
3. Calculate position size based on stop loss
4. Never exceed calculated size
5. Track results
6. Adjust only after 50+ trades
