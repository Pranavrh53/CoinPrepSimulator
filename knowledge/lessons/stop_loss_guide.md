# Stop Loss Orders: Your Safety Net

**Category:** Risk Management  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Reading Time:** 7 minutes

## What is a Stop Loss?

A stop loss is an automatic sell order that triggers when the price drops to a specific level. It's designed to limit your losses on a trade.

**Simple analogy:** It's like a parachute - you hope you never need it, but you ALWAYS wear one.

## Why Stop Losses Matter

### Without Stop Loss:
```
You buy Bitcoin at $50,000
Price drops to $45,000 (-10%)
You think "it'll recover"
Price drops to $40,000 (-20%)
Still holding...
Price drops to $30,000 (-40%)
PANIC SELL at huge loss
```

### With Stop Loss at 5%:
```
You buy Bitcoin at $50,000
Set stop loss at $47,500 (5% down)
Price drops to $47,500
Auto-sells, you lose 5%
You preserve 95% of capital
You can trade another day
```

## How to Set a Stop Loss

### The 2-5% Rule (Beginner)
- Never risk more than 2-5% per trade
- If you have $10,000, risk max $200-500
- Calculate: Entry Price × (1 - 0.05) = Stop Loss

### Example:
```
Capital: $10,000
Risk tolerance: 3% = $300 max loss
Buy Bitcoin at $50,000
Stop loss: $50,000 - ($300/amount) = ?

Better approach:
Stop loss at $48,500 (3% below entry)
Position size: Don't invest more than allows 3% loss
```

## Common Mistakes

### ❌ Setting Stop Loss Too Tight
- Price naturally fluctuates
- You get "stopped out" too early
- Miss potential recovery

### ❌ No Stop Loss At All
- "I'll watch it and sell manually"
- Emotional decisions take over
- Much bigger losses

### ❌ Moving Stop Loss Down
- Price approaches your stop loss
- You panic and move it lower
- "Give it more room"
- This defeats the entire purpose!

## Advanced: Trailing Stop Loss

A trailing stop follows the price up but not down.

**Example:**
```
Buy at $50,000
Set 10% trailing stop
Price rises to $60,000
Stop automatically moves to $54,000 (10% below)
Price rises to $70,000
Stop moves to $63,000
Price drops to $63,000
Auto-sells at $63,000
Profit: $13,000 instead of $10,000!
```

## Psychology: Why Traders Avoid Them

- "I don't want to lock in a loss"
- "It might recover"
- "Stop losses always get hit"
- "I'm watching it closely"

**Reality:** These thoughts have destroyed more accounts than market crashes.

## Your Action Plan

1. **Always** use stop losses
2. Set it **immediately** after buying
3. Calculate the **percentage** first
4. **Never** move it down (only up)
5. Accept small losses to avoid big ones
